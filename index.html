Agentic Spolia

Demo · Work in Progress

Three 20-metre balcony railings on the north facade of a residential building, filled with hundreds of salvaged objects — stone, ceramic, metal, glass, and found materials sourced from European marketplaces.

An AI procurement agent (Antoine) autonomously scouts, evaluates, negotiates, and purchases physical materials from platforms like Ricardo.ch, eBay.de, Leboncoin.fr, and Marktplaats.nl. Each acquisition appears here in real time as a 3D object on the wall.

Composition Rules

Five rules govern slot assignment. Violations show as red wireframes when the Rules overlay is active (C):

  1. No same subcategory adjacent — two ceramic tiles cannot be neighbours
  2. No same category adjacent — two CAT-A objects cannot be neighbours
  3. CAT-D must be surrounded — plastic/digital debris flanked by stone or metal on all sides
  4. Depth variation ≥20mm — adjacent objects must differ in depth for surface relief
  5. Fire zone compliance — Zone 1 (0–3m): all materials. Zone 2 (3–6m): no CAT-D unless encapsulated. Zone 3 (6m+): only CAT-A and CAT-B
Wall Algorithm

~8,000 slots per railing are generated by a bitmap-based free-packing algorithm — no rows, no grid, no patterns. Objects of random size (25–350mm) are placed into a 5mm-resolution bitmap. The result is an irregular organic mosaic like real spolia walls. 5% of objects protrude up to 800mm for dramatic depth.
